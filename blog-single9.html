Manipulative Information and Multimedia

1. As a student, how can manipulative information and media affect you?
- The circulation of information in today’s age has mostly been due to the large popularity of the internet. So if there is an unexpected surge of misinformation being distributed around the world, it would massively affect what we, students, learn on a daily basis. The children of this generation would suffer from the lies that they themselves aren’t even aware of in the first place. We would stay ignorant to the truth and go forward with our lives thinking what information we caught was true.

2. Your generation is considered digital natives. How is multimedia information relevant to you and your future?
- Our generation has grown up and lived on the internet. It is one of the major influences in our lives and will continue to influence us for the rest of it. This information has given us the opportunity to discover new cultures aside from what we are accustomed to and be able to socially interact with other people of various experiences, races, and cultures. This has oversaturated the amount of information that has been laid out in the internet that it is getting harder and harder to discern what are the important ones, the truthful ones, and the lying ones. Going forward, the internet is going to take part in most of our lives and affect the information we get as not only big companies that take your data give you false information but also individuals who have an agenda.

3. How would you evaluate the reliability and validity of multimedia information?
- For my personal experience, I take any information with a grain of salt when it comes to the internet. The information I trust are the ones that come from trustworthy sources and have confirmed their reliability in the information industry. But other than that, I would say that most of the information on the internet is rather fake or staged. I am especially talking about the Tiktoks and the “real” stories that people have to share on Reddit, Facebook, and Twitter. Most of them are trying to either bait for likes and clout or just seeking attention.
